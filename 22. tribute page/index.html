At the age of 20, he ascended to the throne of the Moutong Kingdom in 1877, earning the title Pua Darawati. Tombolotutu played a key role in leading his people against the Dutch, who deployed their elite Marsose forces, a unit known for its involvement in the Diponegoro and Aceh Wars, to suppress his rebellion. His legacy of defiance is chronicled in the book Bara Perlawanan di Teluk Tomini. Although efforts to have Tombolotutu recognized as a national hero began in the 1990s, they faced challenges due to the lack of primary documents. However, on November 10, 2021, President Joko Widodo officially granted him the title of National Hero of Indonesia, alongside other figures like Aji Muhammad Idris and Usmar Ismail.
